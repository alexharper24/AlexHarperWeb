Care Plan & Build Terms

The plain-language version of how a build and a monthly care plan work, so we both know what to expect.

Last updated: [Month Day, Year]

A note: this is written in plain English to set clear expectations, not as formal legal advice. It is a starting point. Before you rely on it as a binding contract, have it reviewed by a lawyer licensed in your state. Items in [brackets] are placeholders for you to set.

1. Who this is between

These terms cover the website work provided by [Alex Harper / legal or business name] ("I", "me") for you, the client ("you"). They apply to the one-time build and to any monthly care plan (Lookout, Tend, or Partner) you choose. When you approve a quote or start a plan, you agree to these terms.

2. The build (one-time)

The build is the one-time work to design and launch your site. Unless your quote says otherwise, it includes design, build, domain and DNS setup, a contact form, and email pointed correctly.

  • I give you a flat quote before any work begins.
  • The build includes [two] rounds of revisions on the design. Further changes are quoted separately.
  • A deposit of [50%] is due to start, with the balance due before the site goes live.
  • The build is considered complete when the site is live and you have confirmed it, or after [14 days] with no requested changes.

3. Care plans (monthly)

Care plans keep your site online and looked after. Each plan lists what it includes on its own page (Lookout, Tend, Partner), and those descriptions are part of these terms.

Included updates are meant for the normal upkeep of your site. Reasonable, ongoing use is expected and welcome. Unusually large or bulk work is quoted as a separate project first. Anything listed as not included on your plan page, or any new feature, page volume, or redesign beyond your plan, is available as a one-off project with its own flat quote.

4. Requests and turnaround

You send requests by email or text. Turnaround times shown on the plan pages are honest targets, not guarantees, and depend on the size of the request and the information you provide. If your site goes down, I treat it as urgent regardless of plan and address it as soon as I can.

5. Billing and payment

  • Care plans are billed [monthly, in advance] by [invoice / card].
  • Payment is due within [7 days] of the invoice date.
  • If payment is more than [15 days] late, I may pause work and, after notice, suspend the site until the balance is paid.
  • I may change plan pricing with at least [30 days] written notice. Your build price never changes after it is quoted.

6. Your domain, hosting, and accounts

Your domain is registered in your name and remains yours. Your website content is yours.

Sites are hosted on trusted third-party platforms (for example GitHub Pages, Azure Static Web Apps, or Cloudflare). Their uptime and availability are outside my control, and I am not responsible for outages caused by those providers or by your domain registrar. I keep reasonable backups but do not guarantee against all data loss.

7. Content and your responsibilities

You provide the text, images, and other materials for your site, and you confirm you have the right to use them. You agree not to ask for content that is unlawful, infringing, or harmful. You are responsible for the accuracy of information on your site (prices, hours, claims).

8. Ownership and intellectual property

When the build is paid in full, you own the final delivered site: your content, text, images, and the page layouts produced for you, to use for your business.

I keep ownership of the underlying tools, code components, and techniques I reuse across projects, and I may reuse them elsewhere. I may also show your site as an example in my portfolio and link to it, unless you ask me in writing not to.

9. Third-party services and costs

Some things carry their own cost: domain registration and renewal, premium services, fonts, or stock images. These are billed to you at cost, with no markup, and I will tell you before incurring anything beyond normal hosting.

10. Cancellation and ending a plan

  • The current month is not refunded once it has started.
  • On cancellation, I provide a copy of your current site files, and your domain stays with you.
  • If your hosting was set up under my account, I will help move it to your own account where possible.
  • I may end a plan immediately for non-payment or for requests that are unlawful or abusive.

The work is provided on a reasonable-effort basis. I do not guarantee specific business results, search rankings, traffic, or sales. To the fullest extent allowed by law, I am not liable for indirect or consequential losses (such as lost revenue), and my total liability is limited to the fees you paid me in the prior [3] months.

Beyond the commitments written here, the services are provided "as is" without other warranties. I will fix genuine defects in my own work that you report within a reasonable time.

I may update these terms occasionally. For meaningful changes to an active plan, I will give you at least [30 days] notice. Continuing your plan after that means you accept the update.
